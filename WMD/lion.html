The Lion

King of the Jungle

About the Lion

The lion (Panthera leo) is one of the most iconic animals in the world and a symbol of strength and courage. As the only truly social cat species, lions live in groups called prides, which typically consist of related females, their cubs, and a small number of adult males.

Physical Characteristics

Male lions are easily recognized by their magnificent manes, which can range from blonde to black. They are the second-largest living cat species after tigers, with males weighing up to 250 kg (550 lbs) and females up to 180 kg (400 lbs).

Interesting Facts

  • Lions can sleep up to 20 hours a day
  • A lion's roar can be heard from up to 5 miles away
  • Lionesses do most of the hunting for the pride
  • Lions are the only cats that live in groups
  • A lion's mane can grow up to 16 inches long

Habitat and Range

Lions once roamed across most of Africa and parts of Europe and Asia. Today, they are found primarily in sub-Saharan Africa, with a small population in India's Gir Forest. They prefer savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands.

Conservation Status

Lions are currently listed as Vulnerable on the IUCN Red List. Their population has declined by approximately 43% over the past 21 years, primarily due to habitat loss, human conflict, and poaching.
